Imagine a compass for real happiness—not one that just tells you to "be happy," but one that shows you how to build a full and meaningful life. This is exactly what the PERMA model proposes. It is a theory of well-being developed by psychologist Martin Seligman, considered one of the founders of positive psychology.

Unlike approaches that only measure how "happy" you feel in the moment, PERMA suggests that our well-being is composed of five pillars. These work together like interconnected puzzle pieces to create a life of greater satisfaction, purpose, and fulfillment.
The name PERMA is an acronym for the five pillars:

• P – Positive Emotions:
This is about feeling good in the here and now: joy, gratitude, pride, serenity, hope, and more. Experiencing positive emotions frequently helps your mind and body function better, makes you more resilient, and improves your quality of life.

• E – Engagement:
Being completely immersed in an activity that absorbs you to the point where you lose track of time. This is often referred to as being in a state of "flow."

• R – Relationships:
Humans are social beings. Deep connections with others—friends, family, or partners—are a fundamental pillar of well-being.

• M – Meaning:
Having a higher purpose: the feeling that what we do matters and that we are part of something bigger than ourselves.

• A – Accomplishment:
Achieving goals that matter to you and feeling a sense of progress, mastery, or personal growth.
One of the most powerful aspects of the PERMA model is that it isn't based on intuition, but on scientific research within positive psychology. Each of the five components is associated with measurable results, such as:
• Greater life satisfaction
• Better physical and mental health
• Higher engagement at work and in studies
• Stronger, healthier personal relationships

Furthermore, PERMA goes beyond simply "seeking positive emotions." It includes aspects like purpose and achievement, which are essential for living a life that feels significant.

A day following the PERMA model wouldn’t necessarily be a "perfect" day, but the model gives you the tools needed to face any difficulties that arise with the best possible attitude.

The PERMA model doesn't promise a life without hardships, but it does promise a richer, more meaningful life aligned with what truly matters. It serves as a guide, reminding us that it isn't enough to just "be happy"—true well-being emerges when we combine positive emotions, engagement, healthy relationships, purpose, and personal accomplishments.
